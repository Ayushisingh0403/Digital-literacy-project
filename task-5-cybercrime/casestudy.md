Case Study: Cyberbullying

Cyberbullying is a form of online harassment where individuals are targeted through social media, messaging apps, or other digital platforms. It often involves sending abusive messages, spreading rumors, sharing embarrassing content, or excluding someone intentionally.

A typical case begins when a conflict or personal dislike arises. The bully may start by posting negative comments or sending hurtful messages. Gradually, the behavior escalates—private photos or screenshots may be shared without consent, fake accounts might be created to mock the victim, and others may be encouraged to join in. This repeated harassment makes it difficult for the victim to escape, as it continues across platforms and at all times.

Teenagers and young adults, especially school and college students, are the most common targets because of their high social media use and sensitivity to peer opinion. Victims often feel isolated, anxious, and powerless.

The consequences of cyberbullying can be severe. It can lead to mental health issues such as stress, depression, low self-esteem, and in extreme cases, self-harm. Academically, victims may lose focus or avoid school altogether. For perpetrators, there can be disciplinary actions, legal consequences, and damage to their reputation.

Preventing cyberbullying requires awareness, responsible online behavior, and prompt reporting of abusive content. Support from peers, parents, and institutions plays a crucial role in addressing and reducing such incidents.
